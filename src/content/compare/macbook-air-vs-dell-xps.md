## Our Verdict

The **MacBook Air M2** wins this comparison for most college students. The battery life alone is transformative — you can leave the charger in your dorm and go through a full day of classes without anxiety. The silent fanless design means you can work anywhere without bothering those around you.

That said, the **Dell XPS 13** is the right choice if you're in an engineering or STEM program that requires Windows-specific software like MATLAB, SolidWorks, or certain chemistry tools. It's also a better buy if you need 16GB of RAM out of the box without paying Apple's upgrade premium.

## Performance

In everyday tasks — writing papers, browsing, video calls, light coding — both laptops feel snappy. The M2 chip's efficiency architecture means it handles these tasks with almost no battery drain. Under sustained load (video editing, compiling code), the XPS 13 has to spin up its fan, while the MacBook stays silent and cool.

## The RAM Trap

Apple's base MacBook Air M2 ships with 8GB of unified memory, which felt adequate in 2022 but feels tight in 2024 with Chrome open alongside Notion and Zoom. We strongly recommend the 16GB upgrade (+$200) if you plan to keep this laptop for 4 years.

Dell ships the XPS 13 with 16GB standard on most configurations — a genuine advantage at similar price points.

## Battery Life in Real Life

We tested both over a full week of student use: class notes, browser research, video streaming, and occasional coding. The MacBook Air averaged **16.5 hours** before needing a charge. The XPS 13 averaged **9 hours**.

On a heavy class day — 8 AM to 8 PM — the MacBook makes it without a charger. The XPS 13 doesn't.

## Who Should Buy What

**Buy the MacBook Air M2 if:** You're in humanities, business, CS, or any major that doesn't require Windows-only software, and you want the best battery life and build quality.

**Buy the Dell XPS 13 if:** Your program requires Windows, you need more RAM at a lower price, or you prefer Windows.
