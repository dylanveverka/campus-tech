## How Much RAM Do Engineering Students Need?

**16GB is the minimum for serious engineering work.** Running MATLAB with large matrix operations, SolidWorks with a complex assembly, or a development environment with multiple services open simultaneously all push past 8GB quickly. A laptop with 8GB of RAM will work, but it will page to disk under load and you'll feel it.

**32GB for heavy CAD and simulation work.** Finite element analysis, computational fluid dynamics, and complex multi-body simulations benefit significantly from 32GB. If you're in mechanical, aerospace, or civil engineering and your coursework involves ANSYS, Abaqus, or similar tools, budget for 32GB if possible — or buy a laptop with user-upgradeable RAM (the ThinkPad X1 Carbon) and add it later.

## Do Engineering Students Need a Dedicated GPU?

It depends on your discipline.

**Yes, a dedicated GPU matters for:** CAD (SolidWorks, CATIA, Fusion 360), rendering and visualization, finite element analysis with GPU acceleration, any machine learning coursework, and game development courses. The RTX 4060 in the Zephyrus G14 and the RTX 4050/4060 in the XPS 15 handle all of these.

**No, integrated graphics is sufficient for:** pure software engineering, most electrical engineering coursework, data analysis in Python or R without GPU-accelerated libraries, and MATLAB without GPU-specific toolboxes. The ThinkPad X1 Carbon and MacBook Pro's integrated graphics handle these use cases without limitation.

## Mac or Windows for Engineering?

**Windows for:** AutoCAD, SolidWorks, CATIA, ANSYS, Simulink, and any department-distributed tools. These are Windows-native applications and macOS doesn't run them without workarounds. If your major has mandatory Windows software — and most mechanical, civil, and electrical engineering programs do — a Mac is the wrong platform.

**Mac for:** Software engineering, CS, data science, and any engineering discipline whose toolchain is terminal-native. Python, Git, Docker, compilers, shell scripting, and web development all work better or equally well on macOS compared to Windows. The battery life advantage of the M3 MacBook Pro is significant for students who code all day.

When in doubt: check your department's required software list in the first week of your program and confirm before purchasing. One mandatory Windows-only application changes the entire decision.

## Software Engineering vs Mechanical Engineering Laptop

**Software engineering:** Prioritize RAM (16GB minimum, 32GB better), CPU core count for compilation, and battery life. A dedicated GPU is optional. The MacBook Pro M3 and ThinkPad X1 Carbon are the strongest picks — the MacBook for battery and performance, the ThinkPad for Windows compatibility and keyboard quality.

**Mechanical engineering:** Prioritize dedicated GPU (SolidWorks and CAD tools need it), RAM (16–32GB), and display quality for reading technical drawings. The Dell XPS 15 and ASUS ROG Zephyrus G14 are the stronger picks — the XPS 15 for the OLED display and balanced design, the Zephyrus for more GPU power at a lower price.
